Protecting your trade:

Married Put – is like purchasing insurance- you purchase a Put on a stock that you're trading to protect against a depretiation on the stock's price. These will potentially unlimit your profits while limiting your loss. If stock price declines before expiration of the option – you can eather exercise your Put option and sell your stock at the stroke price that you chose, Or you could sell your Puts for a profit.

Put option gives you the right but not the obligation to sell the underlined security at a specified stroke price.

Each Put controles 100 shares of underlying security, so if you are trading 600 shares of stock – you would want to buy 6 at the money Put contracts to protect your trade. This is like purchasing an insurance against your stock. It reduces the risk yet still allowing unlimited gains.

If you are going Long a stock and you buy 1000 shares of that stock – you would samultaniously buy 10 Put contracts at the stroke price that you purchased your shares at. So even if the stock drops in price, even if to 0, you still have the right to sell your stock at the exact price you purchased them at any time before expiration of your Put contract. The Married Put contract is a very powerful tool as you can see.
